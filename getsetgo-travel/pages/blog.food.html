Save a buck while enjoying north eatern delicacies.

April 06, 2022

Food always stays at the top of the mind of travelers. It is hard to explore the traditional and culture of a place without tasting the local cuisine. The trouble is, in most countries the cost of food drains a hefty portion of the budget. That is why travelers who travel on a shoestring budget always try to cook their food themselves.

However, while planning a trip to northeast India, you donâ€™t have to worry about any of that. The exotic dishes of these states are tasty as well as cheap. Explore the dishes that the street side shops cater to get a glimpse of the eating habit of the locals while tasting delicious dishes.

The best thing about these street-side dishes of northeast India is that they are all made with fresh and locally available ingredients, following the recipes that are treasured over generations. So, if you are traveling to the North Eastern States of India and want to eat cheap local dishes, check out our list.

Masor Tenga: An Assamese Delicacy

The word tenga literally means sour in Assamese, and the fish curry is true to its name. It is generally cooked with the souring ingredients like dried mangosteen, tomatoes, raw mango, lemon and elephant apple. While this description might scare you, masor tenga is a lightly spiced fish curry with a tangy kick that anyone can enjoy greatly with rice during dinner or lunch.

Traditionally a summer dish, masor tenga is believed to boost digestion after a heavy meal. The different tempering ingredients can give this dish very different taste and can offer a refreshing yet filling variety to the demanding food lovers across the world.

Thukpa: The Taste of Arunachal Pradesh

Thukpa is originally a Tibetan dish. However, the dish has traveled far and beyond with the Tibetan people and now has become one of the signature dishes of Arunachal Pradesh. This dish is cooked with fresh vegetables that grow in the hilly region along with meat and flat noodles.

Thenthuk is another variation of this dish that you can try while you travel in Arunachal Pradesh. The only difference between Thukpa and Thenthuk is that the latter is cooked with hand-pulled noodles instead of the fat ones. This is an amazing dish that will warm your heart even in the middle of the freezing winter.

Kelli Chana: Manipuri Spicy Peas

Kelli Chana is a popular street food that you can find in the North Eastern States of India. The dish is prepared with chickpeas and different spices and herbs. The dish is generally served on a lotus leaf which offers a fresh and unique touch to the dish.

Kappa: A Tourist Favorite of Meghalaya

You cannot miss locals as well as the travelers enjoying a fresh bowl of Kappa on the roads and alleys of Shillong. The soupy snack is perfect for the dewy weather of Meghalaya. The fresh taste of the soup generates from the fresh garden spices that are added to it.
